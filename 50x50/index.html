Recovering the Classics presents

50x50

We want to exhibit at least 50 Recovering the Classics book covers in all 50 states - in a campaign called 50x50.

We've already exhibited these beautiful designs in New York, Boston, San Francisco, Chicago and Washington DC, and now we want them to come to you.

We'd love for libraries, schools, book stores, galleries, local civic organizations, and others across the country to create their own events around the covers. Whether it's exhibits, readings, school activities, or design-a-thons.

And we want local artists and students to submit their own covers, so that each local exhibit will be unique.

50x50 @ NYPL

We held a pop-gallery of 50 covers in the glamorous Salomon Room of the New York Public Library during BookExpo America to celebrate our new partnership with the White House, NYPL and Digital Public Library of America!

Hosts included NYPL, the National Book Foundation, DPLA, The Harvard Book Store, TinyLetter, Litographs, and Litbreaker among many more.

Our guests included several hundreds folks from media and publishing -- including the chief operating officer of Macmillan's trade division, an executive vice president of The New York Times, and many folks from the BookExpo America convention. Live music was provided by the talented Daniel Park.

50x50 @ GitHub in San Francisco

We threw a soiree and pop-up gallery for 35 covers at the fabulous GitHub headquarters in SoMa Neighborhood of San Francisco.

Our literary cocktail menu included Tequila Mockingbird, Catcher in the Rye, Vermouth the Bell Tolls and Margarita Atwood (Margaret Atwood retweeted the menu!).

And we offered a table filled with literary schwag from Out of Print and Litographs. Daniel Park provided live music.

ALA Convention 2015

During the annual convention of the American Library Association in San Francisco, we put together a massive exhibit of over 100 book covers, large and small, in the second floor of the Moscone West convention center.

Over five days, we gave out small postcards to everyone who wanted them. And librarians could earn big posters for taking pictures of the covers and sharing with the #recoveringtheclassics hashtag. Most fun, we had a game where people were given 5 book covers and asked to sort them into chronological order. The prize? One of the big giant posters!

Host Your Own

Do you want to have your own local Recovering the Classics exhibit? If you pair our covers art with local artists, we can send you posters in different sizes, as well as postcards and stickers.

We are working on making these costs reasonable, which is where our Kickstarter comes in.

If no single local organization can exhibit 50 covers, we can send two to five different groups within a state 25 or 10 covers at a time.

As long as there are at least 50 distinct covers in a single state!
